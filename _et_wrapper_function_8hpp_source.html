21// evapotranspiration (ET) module,
22// Version 1.0 by Fred L. Ogden, NOAA-NWS-OWP, May, 2020.
23//
24// includes five different methods to clculate ET, from Chow, Maidment & Mays Textbook, and UNFAO Penman-Monteith:
25// 1. energy balance method
26// 2. aerodynamic method
27// 3. combination method, which combines 1 & 2.
28// 4. Priestley-Taylor method, which assumes the ratio between 1 & 2, and only calculates 1.
29// 5. Penman-Monteith method, which requires a value of canopy resistance term, and does not rely on 1 or 2.
30//
31// This subroutine requires a considerable amount of meteorological data as input.
32// a. temperature and (relative-humidity or specific humidity) and the heights at which they are measured.
33// b. near surface wind speed measurement and the height at which it was measured.
34// c. the ambient atmospheric temperature lapse rate
35// d. the fraction of the sky covered by clouds
36// e. (optional) the height above ground to the cloud base. If not provided, then assumed.
37// f. the day of the year (1-366) and time of day (UTC only!)
38// g. the skin temperature of the earth's surface, TODO: should come from another module to calc. soil or veg. temp.
39// h. the zero-plane roughness height of the atmospheric boundary layer assuming log-law behavior (from land cover)
40// i. the average root zone soil temperature, or near-surface water temperature in the case of lake evaporation.
41// j. the incoming solar (shortwave) radiation. If not provided it is computed from d,e,f, using an
42// updated method similar to the one presented in Bras, R.L. Hydrology. Requires value of the Linke atmospheric
43// turbidity factor, which varies from 2 for clear mountain air to 5 for smoggy air. According to Hove & Manyumbu
44// 2012, who calculated values over Zimbabwe that varied from 2.14 to 3.71. Other values exist in the literature.
45// TODO: This turbidity factor could be calculated from satellite obs. or maybe NOAA already does this?
46//
47// All radiation calculations needed for 1, 3, 4, and 5 require net radiation calculations at the land surface.
48// the net radiation is calculated using a, c, d, e, f, g, j, plus the Linke turbidity factor, which can be estimate
49// from satellite observations.
50//
51
52// NOTE THE VALUE OF evapotranspiration_params.zero_plane_displacement_height COMES FROM LAND COVER DATA.
53// Taken from: https://websites.pmc.ucsc.edu/~jnoble/wind/extrap/
54//
55//Roughness Roughness Landscape Type
56// Class Length (m)
57//----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
58// 0 0.0002 Smooth water surface
59// 0.2 0.0005 Inlet water
60// 0.5 0.0024 Completely open terrain, smooth surface, e.g. concrete runways in airports, mowed grass, etc.
61// 1 0.03 Open agricultural area without fences and hedgerows and very scattered buildings. Only softly
62// rounded hills
63// 1.5 0.055 Agricultural land with some houses and 8 metre tall sheltering hedgerows with a distance of
64// approximately 1250 metres
65// 2 0.1 Agricultural land with some houses and 8 metre tall sheltering hedgerows with a distance of
66// approximately 500 metres
67// 2.5 0.2 Agricultural land with many houses, shrubs and plants, or 8 metre tall sheltering hedgerows
68// with a distance of approximately 250 metres
69// 3 0.4 Villages, small towns, agricultural land with many or tall sheltering hedgerows, forests
70// and very rough and uneven terrain
71// 3.5 0.8 Larger cities with tall buildings
72// 4 1.6 Very large cities with tall buildings and skyscrapers
73// Roughness definitions according to the European Wind Atlas.
74//
75// According to the UN FAO Penman-Monteith example here:
76// http://www.fao.org/3/X0490E/x0490e06.htm#aerodynamic%20resistance%20(ra)
77// the zero plane roughness length,"d" can be approximated as 2/3 of the vegetation height (H): d=2/3*H.
78// the momentum roughness height "zom" can be estimated as 0.123*H.
79// the heat transfer roughness height "zoh" can be approximated as 0.1 * zom.
80//-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
